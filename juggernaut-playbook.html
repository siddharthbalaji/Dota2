01

Hero Identity & When to Pick

Understand what you are before you learn how to play. Juggernaut is a tempo carry — strongest when he translates a won lane and an early Omnislash into map pressure, not a hero who wants to farm silently for 40 minutes.

Win Condition

Snowball the map

Win lane → hit 6 → pick off isolated targets with Omnislash → convert kills into towers, camps and item timings. Close before enemy carries scale.

Playstyle

Aggressive & mobile

Blade Fury clears and dodges, Bladeform keeps you fast, Battle Fury flash-farms. You are constantly moving between fights, farm and objectives.

Skill Floor / Ceiling

Low floor, high ceiling

Anyone can right-click and spin. The mastery is Omnislash target isolation, spin-dodging, ward micro and mana discipline.

Pick Juggernaut when…

  • Your team lacks a self-reliant carry that wins its own lane with little babysitting.
  • You want a hero that provides early-to-mid pickoff pressure, not just a 40-minute farm plan.
  • The enemy draft has squishy, immobile cores/supports that Omnislash deletes (Sniper, Crystal Maiden, Zeus, Invoker, Lina).
  • You have a support with a reliable stun or slow to set up First Blood and Omnislash.
  • You want built-in survivability — Blade Fury's debuff immunity + Healing Ward means fewer babysitting supports.

Avoid / rethink when…

  • Enemy has stacked illusions/summons (Naga, Phantom Lancer, Meepo, Broodmother, Chen) — Omnislash scatters onto units and creeps.
  • Heavy evasion, physical block, or invulnerability saves (Butterfly stackers, Omniknight's Guardian Angel, OD's Astral) blunt your ultimate.
  • Enemy has Break (Silver Edge, Viper) — turning off Blade Dance and Bladeform strips a huge chunk of your damage.
  • Hard counters like Ursa or Legion Commander are open and your team can't peel.

Where Juggernaut is strong

  • Highest-tier agility growth → free armor, damage and attack speed as you level.
  • Fast base attack time → converts attack speed and crit into real DPS quickly.
  • Built-in defense from Blade Fury (debuff immunity) and Healing Ward (% max-HP heal).
  • Guaranteed burst from Omnislash regardless of items — strong the moment you hit 6.

Where Juggernaut is weak

  • Tiny mana pool (low INT) — the single most common death cause: no mana for Omnislash + spin.
  • Low base armor before items → punished by early physical harass.
  • No self-lockdown — needs Basher/Abyssal or allied CC to hold targets down.
  • Omnislash is conditional — creeps, illusions, evasion and invuln all counter it.

Innate Abilities

Patch 7.41 — Facets removedValve removed Facets from the entire game in Patch 7.41 (March 2026). There is no longer any draft-phase choice on Juggernaut. Bladeform is now a permanent innate that every Juggernaut has, and the old Bladestorm Blade Fury component was deleted. So Juggernaut now runs with two always-on innates: Duelist and Bladeform.

Duelist

Innate · always on

Deals bonus damage to enemies who are facing Juggernaut. The bonus is always active during Omnislash and Swift Slash, so your ultimate always gets the full value.

Coaching cue: chasing a fleeing enemy from behind gives less; attacking into someone who turns to fight — or any Omnislash — triggers the full bonus. Position to attack faces. (Note: the old level-10 "+Duelist amplification" talent was replaced in 7.41.)

Bladeform

Innate · always on (was a facet)

Gains a stacking bonus of base Agility + Movement Speed for every interval you go without taking damage — up to 10 stacks. Base every 2s (the level-10 talent drops it to 1s); the per-stack agility bonus now also scales with your hero level (buffed in 7.41). Taking damage dispels the stacks after a short linger.

Play clean to keep stacks high — avoid needless creep/harass damage while farming and you carry free agility and speed everywhere. It makes you a faster farmer and a relentless chaser, and it's a big reason a won lane snowballs so hard. Break (Silver Edge, Viper) stops new stacks from forming.
04

Abilities — Mechanics & How to Use

Each spell has a "what it does" and a "when a coach wants you to press it." The gap between average and great Juggernauts is almost entirely in these usage windows.

Blade Fury

Q · No-target

A whirling storm of blades that deals magic damage per second to enemies around you for the duration and grants debuff immunity (spell-immunity), plus a strong dispel on yourself when the spin ends. You can move — and auto-attack — while spinning.

Uses: early kill pressure & harass, instant camp/wave clear, farming, and — most importantly — a reactive dodge tool: spinning removes and blocks most targeted disables the instant they'd land.

Not a cure-all: it does not block displacement (Meat Hook, Skewer knock), and several ults pierce it (Doom, Legion's Duel, Dismember, Beastmaster's not, but Pugna's Nether Ward damages you before immunity applies). Aghanim's Shard adds radius + a 35% slow. Talent adds +120 DPS and +45 move speed while spinning.

Healing Ward

W · Summon · Controllable

Summons a fast-moving ward that heals all nearby allies for a percentage of their max HP each second for ~25s. It's controllable — micro it to follow you or hide it.

Uses: sustain through lane harass, top up between camps, and — the big one — heal your whole team mid-fight. Percentage healing scales, so it only gets stronger as everyone buys HP.

It has almost no HP and dies to any hit or AoE. Drop it behind trees / on high ground / at the fight's edge and body-block enemies trying to snipe it. Bind ward control so you can reposition it without stopping your attacks. One point is plenty until everything else is maxed.

Blade Dance

E · Passive

~35% chance to deal bonus critical damage on each attack (pseudo-random). This is the passive that turns your fast attacks + cleave into a genuine right-click threat and scales hard with attack speed and armor reduction.

Scaling engine: crit loves attack speed, cleave (Battle Fury) and armor reduction. Talents make it hit harder (+15% crit damage) and — huge — grant +40% lifesteal on Blade Dance at level 25, turning your right-click into self-sustain.

Omnislash

R · Ultimate · Channeled

Leap to a target and perform a series of slashes over the duration, invulnerable throughout, jumping to random enemies within ~450 units between hits. Your signature pickoff and your best "get out of jail" button.

Uses: delete an isolated squishy target; or use it defensively — the invulnerability dodges a teamfight-ending spell combo. Duelist is always active during it.

Isolation is everything. Nearby creeps, illusions and heroes steal slashes. Cast it in open ground, pull the target away, or use terrain — every wasted slash is damage you needed. Blink in first to guarantee you reach the right hero.

Aghanim's Shard

Upgrade

Upgrades Blade Fury — bigger radius and a 35% move-speed slow on enemies inside. Turns your spin into a catch/chase tool and area control. Situational, usually a later luxury.

Swift Slash (Scepter)

Aghanim's Scepter

Grants a short Omnislash on a separate cooldown. A second burst window, an extra farming/mobility tool, and a mini-escape. A core damage/tempo item this patch (~32 min).

The order in words

Neutral items & enchantments (7.41 system)

Reworked in 7.41The neutral system changed a lot: tier-1 neutrals now drop from 0:00, and enchantments were reworked — the old randomness is gone and the options are now tied to your hero's primary attribute (Agility for you). Several artifacts returned (Dagger of Ristul, Possessed Mask, Cloak of Flames, Witchbane) and new ones were added. Exact best-in-slot picks shift every hotfix, so treat the below as principles and confirm the live options in-client.

What to prioritize on Juggernaut

  • Attack speed & agility — your fast attack + crit converts it directly into DPS.
  • Damage / crit / armor-shred neutrals — anything that multiplies your right-click and Omnislash.
  • Early durability (a lane shield / armor artifact) if you're being physically harassed.
  • A blink or mobility tier-4/5 artifact can add another gap-closer for Omnislash setups.

Enchantments (Agility pool)

  • Because enchantments now key off your primary attribute, you'll be offered agility-oriented options — lean into attack speed / agility / movement.
  • No more gambling: the choices are fixed and consistent, so pick the one that fits the game (tempo/chase vs. raw combat stats).
  • Re-evaluate your enchantment as you tier up — a laning-awareness pick early can become a combat-stat pick once fights start.

Macro Playbook

The part most guides skip. Item and skill order win you nothing if your movement, farm routing and fight selection are wrong. Here's the full map-level game.

Laning phase (0–10 min)

  • Win the lane, don't just survive it. Juggernaut wins most lanes (+1.2% net worth @10 on average). Last-hit greedily, deny when you can, and use Blade Fury to clear the wave for a fast level 2–3.
  • Coordinate First Blood. With a support stun/slow, spin on the enemy offlaner at level 1–2 — Blade Fury's DoT plus lockdown often kills outright.
  • Manage your spin. Don't burn Blade Fury purely to harass and then have no dodge/escape when they collapse. Spin to trade, then respect the counter-gank.
  • Sustain with the ward. Value-point Healing Ward resets you to full between waves so you never have to leave lane.
  • Pull vs push: if you're winning, keep the lane pushing to deny their farm and pressure tower; if harassed, let your support pull to reset equilibrium closer to your tower.
  • Benchmark: aim for Power Treads + a Wraith Band + level 6 by ~6 minutes; start Battle Fury components once boots are done.

Farming patterns & efficiency

  • Battle Fury is the whole plan early. Rush it, then use cleave to clear a camp and a wave almost simultaneously. One Blade Fury empties most camps instantly.
  • Route, don't wander. Chain lane creeps into adjacent jungle camps; pre-stack camps for yourself when you have a spare moment.
  • Use illusions & mobility. Manta illusions farm a second spot or push a lane while you farm another; with Swift Blink / Boots of Travel you hop between farm zones.
  • Target ~600–800 GPM in the mid-game — but never farm a camp when Omnislash is up and a support is walking alone. Kills > camps for a tempo carry.
  • Keep Bladeform stacks alive (if you drafted it) — farm cleanly, avoid needless creep damage, and you carry free agility + speed everywhere.

Objectives & map control

  • Turn pickoffs into buildings. Every Omnislash kill is a 5v4 window — immediately take the nearest tower or Roshan rather than walking home.
  • Tower gold is your best gold. Push out lanes with cleave to bank tower gold and deny map space.
  • Roshan: you're not a great solo early Rosher, but with Battle Fury + team you contest fine. Take the Aegis on yourself after an item spike so a bad fight doesn't lose the game.
  • Tormentor: grab the Shard once you have a lane presence — Blade Fury slow adds real catch.
  • Vision as a carry: carry sentries/dust to deward the routes you farm and to reveal the invis heroes that kite your Omnislash. Don't rely on supports for your own safety.

Teamfighting — your role

  • You are a pickoff & cleanup carry, not the initiator. Let your team's CC land first, then Blink → Omnislash the key backliner, or right-click the frontline down.
  • Drop Healing Ward before the fight, hidden at the fight's edge/behind terrain, and micro it with you. Not after you're already dying.
  • Blade Fury to dodge the fight-deciding spell (a targeted stun/silence/hex) while you keep DPSing or reposition.
  • Omnislash has two jobs: offensively to delete a target; defensively, the invulnerability dodges an incoming combo that would kill you. Great players save it as a "no" button when needed.
  • Manta mid-fight dispels silences/roots/dust and splits incoming damage across illusions.
  • Never ult into the creep wave or illusions. Reposition two steps into open ground first.

Split-push & tempo

  • With Battle Fury + Manta + Boots of Travel you're an elite split-pusher: spin clears the wave, illusions push, and you escape with Blade Fury or Blink.
  • When a 5v5 is unfavorable, pressure a side lane to force a response, then TP/blink out — you win the map, not the fight.
  • Keep an eye on enemy positions before committing to a deep push; your escape is spin + blink, not invisibility.

Late game & closing

  • You're now a self-sustaining crit-cleave carry with a double ult. Play the 1-3-1 or deathball depending on your team.
  • Watch mana before every commit (low INT bites hardest here) and mind buyback timing — a dead carry with no buyback loses games.
  • Use Omnislash to open (delete their carry) or to survive their initiation — read which the fight needs.
  • Rapier only when you can protect it and it closes the game; otherwise stay tanky (Skadi, Butterfly, lifesteal).

Combos & Mechanics

The hands-on tech that separates good Juggernauts from great ones. Practice these in a lobby until they're muscle memory.

Blink → Omnislash (the pickoff)

Blink to close the gap, land on your target so the first slash is guaranteed, and cast in open ground so creeps/illusions don't steal jumps. This is your bread-and-butter kill.

Omnislash → Basher/Abyssal cleanup

Ult bursts them low; the instant invulnerability ends, bash + right-click to finish before they escape. Abyssal can also lock first, then Omnislash a guaranteed target.

Spin-dodge

Blade Fury's debuff immunity removes and blocks most targeted disables the moment they'd land — practice spinning as a stun/hex is about to hit. Walk out of channels while spinning.

Healing Ward micro

Bind ward control. Drop it behind trees / on high ground, move it with you, and body-block enemies trying to snipe it. A living ward wins fights.

Manta dispel & dodge

Manta cleanses silences, roots, dust and slows, and the illusions split damage. Use it to break out of lockdown or to dodge on-hit effects, then keep attacking.

Swift Slash + Omnislash double

With Aghanim's you have two burst windows. Chain them on a tanky target, or use one to pick off and hold the other for the next threat.

Treads toggle

Toggle to Strength to have the mana for Omnislash + Blade Fury, then back to Agility for the attacks. A mistimed toggle is a missed kill or a failed escape.

Duelist facing

Attacking enemies who face you deals bonus damage — and it's always on during Omnislash. In a duel, force them to fight you head-on rather than trading from behind.

Spin doesn't block everythingBlade Fury will not save you from displacement (Meat Hook, Skewer) and is pierced by Doom, Legion's Duel and Dismember. Pugna's Nether Ward also damages you before immunity applies. Know these before you spin into a "safe" escape.

Hero Counters & How to Beat Them

Every counter has an answer. Learn why each hero is a problem and the concrete play/item that solves it.

Ursa
Fury Swipes stacks aren't stopped by your spin, and Enrage cuts Omnislash damage — he out-duels you in melee.
Answer: never stand and trade; kite with move speed, go tanky (Skadi), and don't feed his Enrage window.
Legion Commander
Duel pierces spell immunity, forces you to attack, and blocks Omnislash — a won Duel can outright kill you.
Answer: Linken's Sphere blocks Duel; hold Manta/BKB; position so you're never the closest target.
Doom
Doom pierces immunity and shuts your whole kit off for a very long duration.
Answer: Linken's Sphere is the premier buy; don't get caught isolated; BKB helps against his follow-up.
Pudge / Clockwerk
Hook and Hookshot are displacement — your spin won't stop the grab, and Dismember pierces immunity.
Answer: Linken's (vs Hook/Dismember), tight positioning, and don't walk through obvious hook angles.
Outworld Destroyer
Astral Imprisonment removes your target mid-Omnislash, and Sanity's Eclipse punishes your low mana pool hard.
Answer: build INT/HP (Skadi), keep your mana topped, and catch him out of position rather than diving a ready OD.
Pugna
Nether Ward damages you for casting (before spin immunity applies), and Decrepify makes your Omnislash target immune to physical.
Answer: kill the ward first, don't spin next to it, and wait out Decrepify before committing your ult.
Evasion cores (PA / WR)
Evasion makes your attacks and Omnislash slashes miss — your entire damage plan whiffs.
Answer: Monkey King Bar for guaranteed true-strike before you commit to fights.
Omniknight / saves
Guardian Angel (and Astral, Ghost forms, damage-block) negate the physical damage of Omnislash entirely.
Answer: Nullifier to mute the save, or bait/wait it out — don't ult into an active immunity.
Chain CC (Shaman / Bane)
Long single-target disables catch you between spins and lock you out of the fight entirely.
Answer: Manta dispel, Linken's, or (rarely) BKB; never walk in first — let CC be spent on someone else.
Break (Silver Edge users)
Break disables Blade Dance crit and Bladeform — stripping a huge share of your damage and stats.
Answer: don't let the Silver Edge carrier sit on you; itemize burst and end fights before Break matters.
Illusion / summon heroes
Naga, Phantom Lancer, Meepo, Brood, Chen — Omnislash scatters onto units and creeps, and they body-block your isolation.
Answer: lean on Battle Fury cleave + MKB right-click, isolate the real hero, and don't waste ult into a swarm.

Itemizing Against Comps

Read the enemy draft and buy accordingly. Your core is flexible after Manta — bend the back half of your build to the problem in front of you.

vs Evasion

PA, Windranger, Butterfly stackers → Monkey King Bar, non-negotiable before the fight.

vs Magic burst / chain CC

Manta (dispel), Eye of Skadi (HP), Linken's, and rarely BKB for uncounterable chains.

vs Single-target lockdown

Doom, Legion Duel, Hex, Hook → Linken's Sphere to eat the opener.

vs Illusions / summons

Lean on Battle Fury cleave + MKB right-click; isolate the real hero, don't ult the swarm.

vs High armor / physical

An armor-shred neutral or Nullifier; reducing armor makes every crit and Omnislash slash bite harder.

vs Heals / Ghost / Eul saves

Nullifier (mute + slow) and Skadi (healing reduction) shut down the escape/heal.

Tips, Tricks & Do / Don't

Do
  • Toggle Treads to Strength before casting Omnislash + spin.
  • Keep a Mango/Wand for emergency mana — mana is your #1 killer.
  • Isolate with Blink so Omnislash single-targets your kill.
  • Save Omnislash as a defensive "no" button when the fight demands it.
  • Drop Healing Ward before a fight and micro it to safety.
  • Carry sentries/dust — deward your farm and reveal kiters.
  • Rush Battle Fury; it funds your entire timing.
  • Buy MKB the moment you see evasion.
Don't
  • Omnislash into a creep wave, illusions, or a clump — slashes scatter.
  • Spin as your only escape and then get caught with it on cooldown.
  • Over-farm through your 12–25 min pickoff window.
  • Ult into an active Guardian Angel / Astral / Ghost / Decrepify.
  • Trade auto-attacks with Ursa or walk into a ready Legion Duel.
  • Forget buyback/mana before a late-game commit.
  • Leave Healing Ward in the open where any AoE kills it.
  • Assume spin blocks hooks, Doom, or Dismember — it doesn't.

  • Turn every kill into a tower/Roshan/objective.
  • Fight on your spikes; farm cleanly through the lulls.

Micro habits

  • Treads toggle + mana check before every ult.
  • Blink to isolate; open ground only.
  • Healing Ward hidden & microed; spin to dodge the key spell.
